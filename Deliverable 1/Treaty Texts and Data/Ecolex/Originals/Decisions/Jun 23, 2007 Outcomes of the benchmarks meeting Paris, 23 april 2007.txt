xc2\xa0 The World Heritage Committee,    \xc2\xa0 Having examined  Document WHC-07/31.COM/7.3,   \xc2\xa0 Recalling  Decisions  29 COM 7C  and  30 COM 9  adopted at its 29th (Durban, 2005) and 30th (Vilnius, 2006) sessions respectively,  \xc2\xa0 Thanking  the Government of the Netherlands for having hosted the meeting of experts, which took place from 2 to 3 April 2007 in Paris, as well as all the experts who contributed to it,   \xc2\xa0 Noting  the results and recommendations of the expert meeting,  \xc2\xa0  Decides  to formally adopt a monitoring framework for World Heritage properties;  \xc2\xa0  Decides  to integrate the monitoring framework into the next revision of the  Operational Guidelines  and to ensure cross referencing for all World Heritage processes;  \xc2\xa0 Specifically requests  for the revision of the  Operational Guidelines  to ensure the link between outstanding universal value and the format for nominations (Annex 5: 4a on present state of conservation and 4b on factors affecting the property);  \xc2\xa0  Further requests  the States Parties, the Advisory Bodies and the World Heritage Centre to establish desired state of conservation in all state of conservation reports to facilitate sound decisions, specifically for inclusion in / removal of properties from the List of World Heritage in Danger;  \xc2\xa0  Urges  the Advisory Bodies and the World Heritage Centre to provide technical guidance on how to draft statements of significance / outstanding universal value\xc2\xa0 and  requests  ICCROM to use the funds, already allocated, for a focussed guidance manual, in consultation with IUCN and ICOMOS, to be published by the end of 2007;  \xc2\xa0 Noting  the prioritised implementation strategy with focus on the application of the monitoring framework to properties on the List of World Heritage in Danger,  requests  States Parties with properties on the List of World Heritage in Danger to prepare a draft Statement of outstanding universal value for these properties,   \xc2\xa0 Further requests  all States Parties, with the Advisory Bodies, to prepare a draft Statement of outstanding universal value for their properties prior to the arrival of a reactive monitoring mission, and to ensure that the draft statements of outstanding universal value be prepared in advance for the next cycle of Periodic Reporting;  \xc2\xa0 Requests  that stakeholders be involved in preparing all reports required under the  World Heritage Convention  (nomination documents, state of conservation reports, periodic reports) in order to ensure full participation in the definition of the values and desired state of conservation of a property;  \xc2\xa0 Recalls  the requirement that at the time of inscription the Committee decision should entail a clear Statement of outstanding universal value with authenticity and/or integrity and  decides  to add the requirements to describe the desired state of conservation;   Notes  confusion around the term &quot;benchmarks&quot; and  requests  instead the use of the terms &quot;desired state of conservation&quot; and &quot;corrective measures&quot; in all state of conservation documents relating to the List of World Heritage in Danger, and  adopts  in principle the format for state of conservation reports in Annex II.
