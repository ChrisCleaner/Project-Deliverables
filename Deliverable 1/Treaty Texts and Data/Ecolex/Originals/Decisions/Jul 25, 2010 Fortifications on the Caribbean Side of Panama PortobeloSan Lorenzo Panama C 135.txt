The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Having examined  Document WHC-10/34.COM/7B.Add, \n 2.  Recalling  Decision  33 COM 7B.140 , adopted at its 33rd session (Seville, 2009), \n 3.  Notes with concern  that the state of conservation report submitted by the State Party lacks sufficient detail to be considered as a full response to issues previously raised, \n 4.  Also notes  the results of the March 2010 joint World Heritage Centre/ICOMOS reactive monitoring mission,  endorses  its recommendations and  urges  the State Party to: \n a) Implement emergency conservation measures at sectors at risk of collapse as identified in the Emergency Plan, \n b) Formulate and fully implement the management plan for the property, \n c) Define the boundaries and buffer zones for each of the inscribed components, including regulatory measures for their management, \n d) Enact a policy for the property for the commitment at all levels for the conservation of the property; \n 5.  Expresses its deep concern  regarding the state of conservation of the property, in particular the significant degradation of the building fabric, limited ongoing preservation initiatives and the general lack of a maintenance programme which directly impacts the Outstanding Universal Value of the property; \n 6.  Requests  the State Party, in consultation with the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies, to develop a proposal for the Desired State of Conservation, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 35th session in 2011;    \n 7.  Also requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1 February 2011 , a report on the state of conservation of the property and on the steps taken to implement the recommendations set out above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 35th session in 2011.
