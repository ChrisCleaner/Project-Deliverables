The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp;   Having examined   Document WHC-13/37.COM/7A.Add, \n 2.&nbsp;   Recalling   Decision   36 COM 7A.35   , adopted at its 36th&nbsp;session (Saint-Petersburg, 2012), \n 3.&nbsp;   Acknowledges   the efforts made by the State Party in addressing conservation concerns at the property and   encourages   it to continue such efforts in cooperation with the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies; \n 4.&nbsp;   Urges   the State Party to develop and approve the Management Plan for the property, including a conservation programme with short, medium and long term priorities, provisions for risk management and provisions for public use, and   requests   it to submit three printed and electronic copies of the draft Management Plan by   1&nbsp;February&nbsp;2014   for review by the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies; \n 5.&nbsp;   Also requests   the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centrefor review by the Advisory Bodies, the technical specifications and details of the projects for large scale drainage at the property and regulation of vehicular traffic at Zamora Street prior to implementation; \n 6.&nbsp;   Reiterates     its request   to the State Party to update, in consultation with the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies, the Desired State of Conservation and the corrective measures for the removal of the property from the List of World Heritage in Danger, as well as a revised timeframe, and to submit a proposal to the World Heritage Centre by   30&nbsp;November&nbsp;2013   for examination by the Advisory Bodies, in view to submit the final proposal to World Heritage Committee at its 38th&nbsp;session in 2014 for approval; \n 7.&nbsp;   Further requests   the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by   1&nbsp;February&nbsp;2014,   an updated report on the state of conservation of the property and the implementation of the above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 38th&nbsp;session in 2014; \n 8.&nbsp;     Decides   to retain Coro and its Port (Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela) on the List of World Heritage in Danger.
