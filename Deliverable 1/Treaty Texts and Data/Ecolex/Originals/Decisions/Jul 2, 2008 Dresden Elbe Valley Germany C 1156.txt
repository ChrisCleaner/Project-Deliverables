The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Having examined  Document  WHC-08/32.COM/7A , \n 2.  Recalling  Decisions  30 COM 7B.77  and  31 COM 7A.27  adopted at its 30th (Vilnius, 2006) and 31st (Christchurch, 2007) sessions respectively, and in particular its concern that the construction project of the Waldschl&ouml;sschen Bridge would irreversibly damage the values and integrity of the property in accordance with Paragraph 179 (b) of the  Operational Guidelines , \n 3.  Notes with satisfaction  that the State Party invited a Reinforced Monitoring mission to review the bridge construction project and any alternative solutions; \n 4.  Also notes  the report provided by the Reinforced Monitoring mission of February 2008 confirming that the current bridge project would irreversibly damage the Outstanding Universal Value and integrity of the property; \n 5.  Regrets  the fact that the authorities, having allowed the construction works to proceed, have seriously compromised the Outstanding Universal Value of the property; \n 6.  Expresses its deep concern  that the work planned will irreversibly damage the Outstanding Universal Value and integrity of the property; \n 7.  Strongly requests  the State Party to immediately halt the current construction works and restore the property to its former state of conservation; \n 8.  Strongly urges  the State Party to reconsider the alternative tunnel option; \n &nbsp; \n 9.  Decides  to continue to apply the Reinforced Monitoring mechanism to the property and  requests  the State Party to provide progress reports as relevant; \n 10.  Also requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  1 February 2009 , a report on the state of conservation of the property, including a progress report on the implementation of the issues above-mentioned, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 33rd session in 2009 ; \n 11.  Further decides  to retain the Dresden Elbe Valley (Germany) on the List of World Heritage in Danger, with the deletion of this property from the World Heritage List at its 33rd session in 2009, if the planned works on the bridge continue and the damage already caused is not reversed.
