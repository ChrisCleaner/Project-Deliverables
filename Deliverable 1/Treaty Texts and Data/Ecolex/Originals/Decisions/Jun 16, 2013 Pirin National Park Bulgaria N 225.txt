The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp;   Having examined   Document WHC-13/37.COM/7B.Add, \n 2.&nbsp;   Recalling   Decision     36 COM 7B.18     adopted at its 36th session (Saint-Petersburg, 2012), \n 3.&nbsp;   Notes   that a proposed amendment to the management plan of Pirin National Park which would allow further skiing developments within the buffer zone of the property, is currently under consideration by the State Party; \n 4.&nbsp;   Requests   the State Party to ensure that the proposed amendment is in line with the 2010 Strategy for Sustainable Nature Tourism and that an appropriate monitoring mechanism is put in place, as requested by the Committee and the 2011 Reactive monitoring mission, before approval of the proposed amendment; \n 5.&nbsp;   Also requests   the State Party to undertake a Strategic Environmental Assessment of the development of the buffer zone, including consultations with stakeholders, and   urges   the State Party to ensure that these proposals will not negatively impact the Outstanding Universal Value (OUV) and in particular the integrity of the property; \n 6.&nbsp;   Reiterates its request   to the State Party to confirm that no further ski development inside the property will be permitted and   recalls its position   that if any additional development of ski facilities, ski runs, or associated infrastructure within the property are undertaken, the conditions for inscription of the property on the List of World Heritage in Danger will be fulfilled; \n 7.&nbsp;   Urges   the State Party to expedite the implementation of the outstanding recommendations of the 2011 joint World Heritage Centre/IUCN reactive monitoring mission to the property which have not been fully implemented to date; \n 8.&nbsp;   Further requests   the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by   1 February 2014   , a report on the state of conservation of the property and the implementation of the above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 38th session in 2014.
