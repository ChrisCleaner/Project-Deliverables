Property:  The Historic Centre (Chor&aacute;) with the Monastery of Saint-John the Theologian and the Cave of the Apocalypse on the Island of P&aacute;tmos  \n Id. N&deg;:  942  \n State Party:  Greece    \n Criteria:  C (iii) (iv) (vi)  \n The Committee inscribed the site on the World Heritage List on the basis of  criteria (iii), (iv)  and  (vi) : \n   Criterion (iii):   The town of Chor&aacute; on the Island of P&aacute;tmos is one of the few settlements in Greece that have evolved uninterruptedly since the 12th century. There are few other places in the world where religious ceremonies that date back to the early Christian times are still being practised unchanged. \n   Criterion (iv):   The Monastery of Hagios Ioannis Theologos (Saint John the Theologian) and the Cave of the Apocalypse on the Island of P&aacute;tmos, together with the associated medieval settlement of Chor&aacute;, constitute an exceptional example of a traditional Greek Orthodox pilgrimage centre of outstanding architectural interest. \n   Criterion (vi):   The Monastery of Hagios Ioannis Theologos and the Cave of the Apocalypse commemorate the site where St John the Theologian (Divine), the "Beloved Disciple", composed two of the most sacred Christian works, his Gospel and the Apocalypse. \n The Delegate of Thailand raised the question of eligibility of criterion (vi). He thought that the criterion (iii) should be applied. This recommendation was also endorsed by ICOMOS and the Committee. Delegates and observers commended the high values of the site and decided to keep the criterion (vi). \n &nbsp;
