The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Having examined  Document  WHC-08/32.COM/7B , \n 2.  Recalling  Decision  31 COM 7B.74 , adopted at its 31st session (Christchurch, 2007), \n 3.  Notes  the progress made by the State Party and its commitment to set up a Coordinating Committee for the property; \n 4.  Notes with concern  further new and inappropriate development proposals between the Afrosiab and Timurid city for the re-creation of the Timurid city walls, and a new hotel with "historic fa&ccedil;ades" near the city walls; \n 5.  Urges  the State Party to continue the development of the management plan, including appropriate zoning and other matters identified at its 31st session (Christchurch, 2007); \n 6.  Also urges  the State Party to develop an overall strategic approach to the property\'s conservation to be agreed to by stakeholders through the adoption of the management plan, and to submit, to the World Heritage Centre, in conformity with Paragraph 172 of the  Operational Guidelines , information about any major project proposals; \n 7.  Decides  to implement the Reinforced Monitoring mechanism to the property and asks in particular that the Committee be informed on the results on any information relevant for the conservation of the Outstanding Universal Value of the property; \n 8.  Also requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre by  1 February 2009 , a report on the state of conservation of the property, including progress made in: \n a) finalizing the management plan, \n b) developing the conservation plan, \n c) documenting historic features (inventories and surveys), \n d) strengthening the Coordinating Committee, \n e) developing proposed zoning and road schemes including proposals to close the new road between Afrosiab and Timurid city to through-traffic, \n for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 33rd session in 2009.
