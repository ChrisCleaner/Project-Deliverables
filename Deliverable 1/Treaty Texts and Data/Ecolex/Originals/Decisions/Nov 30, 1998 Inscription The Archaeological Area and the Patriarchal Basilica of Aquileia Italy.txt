N&deg;:  825   \n State Party:  Italy     \n Criteria:  C (iii)(iv)(vi)   \n The Committee inscribed the site on the World Heritage List on the basis of  criteria (iii), (iv), and (vi) : \n  Criterion (iii) : Aquileia was one of the largest and most wealthy cities of the Early Roman Empire. \n  Criterion (iv) : By virtue of the fact that most of ancient Aquileia survives intact and unexcavated, it is the most complete example of an Early Roman city in the Mediterranean world. \n  Criterion (vi) : The Patriarchal Basilican Complex in Aquileia played a decisive role in the spread of Christianity into central Europe in the early Middle Ages. \n &nbsp;
