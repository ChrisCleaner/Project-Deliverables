The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Having examined  Document WHC-12/36.COM/7A, \n 2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Recalling  Decision  35 COM 7A.33,  adopted at its 35th session (UNESCO, 2011), \n 3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Welcomes  the progress made by the State Party in implementing the recommendations of the 2010 reactive monitoring mission; \n 4.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Adopts  the following Desired state of conservation for the removal of the property from the List of World Heritage in Danger, as follows: \n a)&nbsp; Operational and sustainable management system for the Chan Chan Archaeological Zone in place, including functional institutional arrangements and secured funding, \n b)&nbsp; Approval of revised Management Plan and integration with other planning tools at the municipal and provincial levels, particularly for the management of the buffer zone, \n c)&nbsp; Continued implementation of conservation and maintenance measures at the property, including mitigation measures to address the vulnerability of the earthen architecture remains, \n d)&nbsp; Legislative and regulatory measures to address the issues of illegal occupations and activities at the site enacted and enforced; \n 5.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Also adopts  the following corrective measures and timeframe for their implementation in order to ensure conditions of integrity and authenticity of the property and meet the Desired state of conservation: \n a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Measures to be implemented within one year : \n &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Conservation \n (i) &nbsp;Comprehensive conservation condition assessment and monitoring to assess the existing state of conservation of the property, \n (ii) &nbsp;Identification of priority areas for interventions, \n (iii) Implementation of priority and emergency conservation measures at vulnerable areas of the property, with particular focus on the nine palaces and areas with decorated surfaces, as well as measures centered on the control of water table levels, \n (iv) &nbsp;Definition and adoption of conservation guidelines for intervention, \n (v) &nbsp;Implementation and maintenance of the physical delineation of the property including vegetation barriers and perimeter walls, \n (vi) Comprehensive assessment of the current conditions of the existing site museum, identification of priority emergency measures and definition of a comprehensive intervention programme to be included in the public use plan. \n (vii) Addressing of solid waste management at the boundaries of the site in collaboration with pertinent authorities, \n &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Protection and planning \n (i) Updating of the Management Plan, including a revised risk Management Plan and a public use plan as well as scheduled and costed provisions for the conservation and management of the property and its buffer zone, \n (ii) Finalization of the definition of the buffer zone and its regulatory measures in collaboration with municipal authorities, \n (iii) Dissemination and circulation among stakeholders of updated plans for the property and its buffer zone, including provisions and regulations for each zone. Collaboration with entities in defining regulatory measures for the management of the buffer zone and of the World Heritage property, \n (iv) Finalization of regulations for Law no. 28261 to address fundamental issues such as the illegal removal of soil, agricultural works and the illegal occupation at the property, \n &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Management \n (v) Evaluation of effectiveness of existing institutional arrangements to include revised provisions in the updated Management Plan, \n (vi) Identification of sources for secured funding in the long-term, \n b)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Measures to be implemented within two years  :  \n Conservation \n (i) Continued implementation of conservation and maintenance actions, with particular focus on finalizing interventions at vulnerable areas, \n (ii) Monitoring programme fully in place to evaluate the efficacy and results of interventions and to revise them if needed, \n (iii) Maintenance of the physical delimitation of the site, \n (iv)Measures for solid waste management at the boundaries of the site fully addressed, \n (v) &nbsp;Interventions for public use at the property, particularly in respect to the site museum in accordance to provisions included in the revised Management Plan, \n (vi)Interventions for risk management in accordance to provisions identified in the Management Plan, \n Protection and planning \n (vii) Integration of the Management Plan in territorial and urban development plans, \n (viii) Dissemination of the revised Management Plan to strengthen public and private support in its implementation, \n (ix) Approval / enactment of regulatory measures for Law No. 28261 to ensure the conservation and protection of the Outstanding Universal Value and conditions of integrity and authenticity of the property, \n (x) Adoption of regulatory measures for the management of the buffer zone, \n &nbsp;Management \n (xi)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Operational management arrangements and budgets secured for the comprehensive implementation of the Management Plan, \n c)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Measures to be implemented within three years  :  \n Conservation \n (i)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Continued implementation of conservation and maintenance measures according to the revised Management Plan, \n (ii)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Continued implementation of the monitoring programme and evaluation of results to adapt measures, \n (iii)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Continued implementation of public use actions according to Management Plan, \n (iv)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Continued implementation of risk management actions according to Management Plan, \n (v)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Conclusion of measures at the existing site museum; \n Protection and planning \n (vi)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Full enforcement of legislative and regulatory frameworks passed by the State Party, \n (vii)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Relocation of illegal settlers in collaboration with pertinent authorities, \n (viii)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Adequate control of encroachments and urban pressure; \n Management \n (ix)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Full and systematic implementation of the revised Management Plan in accordance to prescribed policies, \n (x)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Functional institutional arrangements with adequate resources secured for long-term implementation of the formulated Management Plan;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \n 6. &nbsp; Reiterates its request  to the State Party to finalize the process for the approval of pertinent legislation and regulations for the property and buffer zone to ensure their adequate protection, and to find an urgent solution to the legal issues that have been pending for the last 10 years; \n 7. &nbsp;&nbsp; Also reiterates its request  to the State Party to provide the updated Master Plan, including a public use plan and a comprehensive risk preparedness plan for the property by  1 February 2013 ; \n 8. &nbsp;&nbsp; Requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1 February 2013 , an updated report on the state of conservation of the property and the implementation of the above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 37th session in 2013; \n 9.&nbsp;&nbsp;  Decides    to retain Chan Chan Archaeological Zone (Peru) on the List of World Heritage in Danger.
