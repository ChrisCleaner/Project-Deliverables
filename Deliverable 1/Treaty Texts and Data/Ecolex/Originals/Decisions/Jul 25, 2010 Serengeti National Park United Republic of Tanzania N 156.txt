The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp; Having examined  Document WHC-10/34.COM/7B, \n 2.&nbsp; Recalling  Decision  33 COM 7B.10 , adopted at its 33rd session (Seville, 2009), \n 3.&nbsp; Acknowledges  the progress achieved by the State Party, in collaboration with the Kenyan Government and WWF\'s East Africa Programme, towards formulating sustainable water resource management policies for the Mara River Basin, and  requests  the State Party to ensure that these policies are rapidly put in place; \n 4.&nbsp; Welcomes  the State Party\'s intention to expanding the property to include Speke Gulf, which is a crucial alternative water resource during times of drought; \n 5.&nbsp; Expresses its utmost concern  about the proposed North Road which will dissect the northern wilderness area of the Serengeti over 53 km,  considers  that this proposed alignment could result in irreversible damage to the property\'s Outstanding Universal Value and therefore  urges  the State Party to submit an Environmental Impact Assessment to the World Heritage Centre before a decision to implement the project is taken; \n 6.&nbsp; Also notes  the reports of the significant increase in rhinoceros and elephant poaching within the property, and  requests  the State Party to continue improving its anti-poaching strategies and law enforcement activities; \n 7.&nbsp; Further requests  the State Party to invite a joint World Heritage Centre / IUCN reactive monitoring mission to the property to assess its state of conservation, including potential threats such as the North Road proposal, as well as reports on a significant increase in poaching; \n 8.&nbsp; Requests furthermore  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1 February 2011 , a report on the state of conservation of the property, including on the status of the North Road proposal, sustainable water management policies for the Mara River, and the status of poaching, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 35th session in 2011.
