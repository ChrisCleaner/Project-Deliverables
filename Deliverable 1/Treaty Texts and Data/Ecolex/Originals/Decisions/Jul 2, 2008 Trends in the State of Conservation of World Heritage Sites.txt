The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Having examined  documents  WHC-08/32.COM/7B, WHC-08/32.COM7B.Add  and  WHC-08/32.COM7B.Add2 , \n 2.  Recognizing  that the state of conservation reports are an important tool for sustaining the World Heritage properties; and \n 3.  Noting  the discussion that took place at the 32nd session of the World Heritage Committee; \n 4.  Requests  the World Heritage Centre to: \n a) prepare, after consultation with the Chairperson of the World Heritage Committee, an analytical summary of the state of conservation of the World Heritage discussed at the 32nd session (Qu&eacute;bec City, 2008) identifying trends, by 1st November 2008, for distribution to the Committee members and discussion at the 33rd session in 2009; \n b) identify issues emanating from the analytical summary, in consultation with the Advisory Bodies, to be discussed on relevant agenda items of the Committee, including the Reinforced Monitoring mechanism; \n c) prepare a lexicon of terms and recommendations for their consistent application in state of conservation reports; \n d) provide, where available, a printed Statement of Outstanding Universal Value, for properties on the List of World Heritage in Danger and those discussed for in-Danger listing; \n e) add a link to illustrative material and relevant statements of Outstanding Universal Value, and attribute information to source and date; \n 5.  Also requests  the World Heritage Centre to identify a mechanism for consultation with States Parties during the development of the State of Conservation reports to ensure their accuracy.
