The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Having examined  Document  WHC-08/32.COM/10A , \n 2.  Recalling  Decisions  31 COM 13  and  31 COM 17  adopted at its 31st session (Christchurch, 2007), \n 3.  Thanking  the Government of the United Kingdom for having hosted the Science and Technology expert workshop, which took place from 21 to 23 January 2008 in London, as well as all the experts who contributed to it, \n 4.  Noting  the results and recommendations of the Science and Technology expert workshop within the framework of the Global Strategy, \n 5.  Takes note  of the progress report on the global thematic studies undertaken by ICOMOS and IUCN; \n 6.  Requests  IUCN and ICOMOS to continue to develop their work on thematic studies, in relation to priorities identified in Document  WHC-08/32.COM/10A ; \n 7.  Noting  the underfunding of this significant activity for identifying properties with or having potential Outstanding Universal Value; \n 8.  Takes note  of the need for additional resources to be provided to support priority work by ICOMOS and IUCN on thematic studies, including updating existing studies, the application of criterion VII and new initiatives, and to promote their effective use by States Parties; \n 9.  Invites  States Parties to the  Convention  to consider providing extra-budgetary assistance to support the work outlined in paragraphs 6 and 7 above; \n 10.  Thanks  the government of France for its generous offer to fund a thematic study on Agro-pastoral Cultural Landscapes. \n 11.  Thanks  the government of Spain for its generous offer to fund a thematic study on prehistoric sites;
