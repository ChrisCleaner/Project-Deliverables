The World Heritage Committee; \n 1.  Having examined  Document WHC-10/34.COM/7B, \n 2.  Recalling  Decision  32 COM 7B.41 , adopted at its 32nd session (Quebec City, 2008), \n 3.  Recognizes  the efforts made by the State Party to address the actions requested in Decision  32 COM 7B.41 ; \n 4.  Welcomes  the submission of a draft Statement of Outstanding Universal Value for the property; \n 5.  Thanks  the State Party for proposing a minor modification to include 21 formal reserves within the property that are already covered by the Tasmanian Wilderness World Heritage Area (TWWHA) Management Plan, also  welcomes  its commitment to add the Melaleuca-Cox Bight area to the property once mining licences have expired, and also  recalls  its request regarding the potential for further additional areas to be considered at the discretion of the State Party for eventual addition to the property; \n 6.  Notes  the potential for impact on the integrity of the existing World Heritage property from adjoining forestry operations, and  requests  the State Party to maintain rigorous assessment and management systems to ensure that no such impacts arise; \n 7.  Also requests  the State Party to finalize as soon as possible the creation of a mechanism involving all relevant stakeholders, to monitor, asses and manage the impact of forestry operations, road construction and regeneration on the integrity of the TWWHA, and adjoining reserves, as previously requested by the Committee; \n 8.  Further requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1 February 2012 , an updated report on the state of conservation of the property, especially on the outcomes of the monitoring arrangements focusing specifically on the impact of the logging operations and road construction on the Outstanding Universal Value of the existing property, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 36th session in 2012.
