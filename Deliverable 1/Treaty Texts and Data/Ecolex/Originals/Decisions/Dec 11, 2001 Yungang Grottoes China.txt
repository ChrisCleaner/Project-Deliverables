N&deg;:  1039  \n State Party:  China  \n Criteria:  C (i) (ii) (iii) (iv)  \n The Committee inscribed the Yungang Grottoes on the World Heritage List under criteria (i), (ii), (iii) and (iv): \n  Criterion (i):&nbsp; The assemblage of statuary of the Yungang Grottoes is a masterpiece of early Chinese Buddhist cave art. \n  Criterion (ii):  The Yungang cave art represent the successful fusion of Buddhist religious symbolic art from south and central Asia with Chinese cultural traditions, starting in the 5th century CE under Imperial auspices. \n  Criterion (iii):  The power and endurance of Buddhist belief in China are vividly illustrated by the Yungang grottoes. \n  Criterion (iv):  The Buddhist tradition of religious cave art achieved its first major impact at Yungang, where it developed its own distinct character and artistic power. \n The Committee encouraged the State Party to continue the maintenance efforts and management planning for the site. \n &nbsp;
