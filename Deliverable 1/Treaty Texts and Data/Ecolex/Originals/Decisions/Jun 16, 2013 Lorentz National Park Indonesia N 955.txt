The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp;   Having examined   Document WHC-13/37.COM/7B.Add, \n 2.&nbsp;   Recalling   Decision   3     5     COM 7B.1     5   , adopted at its 35th&nbsp;session (UNESCO, 2011), \n 3.&nbsp;   Takes note   that the State Party did not submit its report by   1&nbsp;February&nbsp;2013   , and   notes   the information provided by the State Party in its report of 22&nbsp;May&nbsp;2013; \n 4.&nbsp;   Notes with serious concern   the State Party&rsquo;s intent to proceed with the construction of the Lake Habema &ndash; Nduga &ndash; Kenyem road without undertaking a Strategic Environmental Assessment of the integrated transport plan for Papua, and   considers   that the continuation of construction of the Lake Habema &ndash; Nduga &ndash; Kenyam road and the proposed Jayapura &ndash; Wamena &ndash; Mulia road, if built through the property would represent a clear potential danger to its Outstanding Universal Value (OUV) in line with paragraph 180 of the   Operational Guidelines   , and be a clear basis for the inscription of the property on the List of World Heritage in Danger; \n 5.&nbsp;   Urges   the State Party to rigorously ensure the protection and conservation of the property&rsquo;s OUV, and prevent the fragmentation of the largely intact wilderness that makes up the property; \n 6.&nbsp;   Requests   the State Party to provide detailed information about the revival of the Trans-Papua Highway plan, and the measures taken to ensure the protection of the property&rsquo;s OUV; \n 7.&nbsp;   Reiterates its request   to the State Party to fully implement the 2008 and 2011 monitoring mission recommendations, and to prioritize the following: \n a)&nbsp; Cease all road construction in the property and rehabilitate recently constructed roads and mitigate their impacts, \n b)&nbsp; Further investigate and address forest die-back, and develop management guidelines for all relevant stakeholders undertaking activities within the property to contain the spread of the die-back disease, \n c)&nbsp; Review the budgeting for the property in order to ensure that resources are directed to address the major threats to its OUV, \n d)&nbsp; Build the capacity of park staff to manage complex ecological, technical and sociological issues; \n 8.&nbsp;   Also requests   the State Party to provide an electronic copy and three printed copies of the Management Plan and zoning plan for review by the World Heritage Centre and IUCN; \n 9.&nbsp;   Further     requests   the State Party to invite an IUCN reactive monitoring mission to the property in order to assess its state of conservation, in particular in relation to impacts from road construction, to assist the State Party with developing a conservation strategy that will ensure the conservation and strict protection of the property&rsquo;s OUV, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 38th session in 2014; \n   10.&nbsp;     Calls upon   the international community to support   the State Party in resolving the severe constraints to the effective operation of the Park management including funding, limited monitoring and surveillance equipment, and limited staff capacity and technical expertise;   \n 11.&nbsp;   Requests furthermore   the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by   1 February 2015   a report on the state of conservation of the property, including progress achieved in implementing the recommendations of the 2008 and 2011 monitoring missions, as well as the results of the Strategic Environmental Assessment (SEA) of the integrated transport programme for Papua Province, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 39th session in 2015   .   \n &nbsp;
