The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp;   Having examined   Document WHC-13/37.COM/7B.Add, \n 2.&nbsp;   Recalling   Decisions   35 COM 7B.107 and 36 COM 7B.86   adopted at its 35th (UNESCO, 2011) and 36th (Saint-Petersburg, 2012) sessions respectively, \n 3.&nbsp;   Regrets   that the State Party did not submit a state of conservation report; \n 4.&nbsp;   Takes note   of the steps the State Party is making towards developing legal measures for the protection of World Heritage cultural properties, as well as for the establishment of a &ldquo;Committee on the conservation of the spiritual, cultural and natural heritage of the Solovetsky Archipelago&rdquo;; \n 5.&nbsp;   Notes   that the State Party decided to organise, further to the Committee&rsquo;s request and in collaboration with the World Heritage Centre, ICCROM and ICOMOS, a training workshop for the religious representatives involved in the management and use of the World Heritage properties in the Russian Federation; \n 6.&nbsp;   Requests   the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre detailed information concerning the Master Plan of the Solovetsky Monastery and any other planning documents prior to the reactive monitoring mission; \n 7.&nbsp;   Reiterates its concern   about the possible reconstruction of the monastery buildings and other major interventions in the landscape of the property in terms of the impact on its Outstanding Universal Value (OUV), and   also requests   the State Party to provide detailed information to the World Heritage Centre prior to the mission; \n 8.&nbsp;   Reiterates its request   to the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre all project proposals that may threaten the OUV of the property, in conformity with Paragraph 172 of the   Operational Guidelines   , as well as to submit alongside all new proposals Heritage Impact Assessments (HIAs), in conformity with the ICOMOS Guidance on HIAs for World Heritage cultural properties; \n 9.&nbsp;   Further reiterates its request   to the State Party to invite, as a matter of urgency, a joint World Heritage Centre/ICOMOS/ICCROM reactive monitoring mission to the property to assess the overall state of conservation of the property and   recommends   that the mission be scheduled as soon as possible; \n 10.&nbsp;   Further requests   the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by   1 February 2014   , a report on the state of conservation of the property and on the implementation of the above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 38th session in 2014.
