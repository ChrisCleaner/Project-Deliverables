The World Heritage Committee, \n \n  Having examined  Document WHC-14/38.COM/7A, \n  Recalling  Decision  37 COM 7A.38  adopted at its 37th session (Phnom Penh, 2013), \n  Commends  the State Party for its long-term commitment and efforts to address the deterioration of the earthen architecture remains of the property and to put in place a sustainable and operational management system to continue to handle decay factors and threats; \n  Considers  that the State Party has made considerable progress in meeting the Desired state of conservation for the removal of the property from the List of World Heritage in Danger and in the implementation of the corrective measures; \n  Notes  however that the corrective measures, which are currently in planning stages, have to be finalized to ensure the long-term protection of the property and  urges  the State Party to:\n \n Finalize the update process of the Management Plan and other planning tools for the property at the municipal and provincial level and provide an electronic and three printed copies of the draft revised planning tools for review by the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies, \n Finalize the approval process of Law 28261 to ensure that the property is adequately protected from illegal occupation; \n \n \n  Requests  the State Party to invite a joint World Heritage Centre/ICOMOS reactive monitoring mission to the property with a view to assess whether the implementation of the above-mentioned actions may warrant the removal of the property from the List of World Heritage in Danger; \n  Also requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1\xc2\xa0February\xc2\xa02015 , an updated report, including a 1-page executive summary, on the state of conservation of the property and the implementation of the above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 39th session in 2015; \n   Decides    to retain Chan Chan Archaeological Zone (Peru) on the the List of World Heritage in Danger.
