The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Having examined  Document WHC-07/31.COM/7B, \n 2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Recalling  Decision  30 COM 7B.32 , adopted at its 30th session (Vilnius, 2006), \n 3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Notes with concern  the issues raised by NGOs in relation to the impacts of logging adjacent to the World Heritage property and the commencement of the North Weld Road which compromises options for future extensions to the World Heritage property; \n 4.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Urges  the State Party to consider the extension of the World Heritage property to include critical old-growth forests to the east and north of the property, or at least to manage these forests in a manner which is consistent with a potential World Heritage value; \n 5.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Expresses its concern  about the risk from fire related to forest regeneration and natural events, and its possible adverse impact on the World Heritage property, and  requests  the State Party to prepare a Risk Management Plan and to consider distancing the logging operations from the boundary of the property; \n 6.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Taking into account  the clarification provided orally by the State Party at the 31st session,  also requests  the State Party to invite a joint World Heritage Centre/IUCN/ICOMOS mission to assess the state of conservation of the property,&nbsp; focusing on: \n a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; appropriate management of areas of heritage value which are currently outside the property, \n b)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; an assessment of the degree of risk related to regeneration fires in areas adjacent to the World Heritage property&nbsp;as well as of the effectiveness of the fire management system in place, \n c)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; impacts of proposed forestry operations (including the construction of new roads) on the outstanding universal value of the property, \n 7.  Requests  the State Party to provide the World Heritage Centre with an updated report by  1 February 2008  on the state of conservation of the property and the above mentioned issues for examination by the Committee at its 32nd session in 2008.
