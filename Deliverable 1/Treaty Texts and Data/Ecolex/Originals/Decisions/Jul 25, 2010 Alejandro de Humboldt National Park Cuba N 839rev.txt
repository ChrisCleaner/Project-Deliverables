The World Heritage Committee; \n 1.&nbsp; Having examined  Document WHC-10/34.COM/7B, \n 2.&nbsp; Recalling  Decision  33 COM 7B.36 , adopted at its 33rd session (Seville, 2009), \n 3.&nbsp; Notes  the progress achieved by the State Party in implementing the 2009 Operational Plan, which has enhanced the effectiveness of management of the property; \n 4.&nbsp; Notes with appreciation  the confirmation provided by the State Party that at present no mining is planned in the property, but  considers  that the continued existence of mining concessions if activated would be considered a threat to the property, as per Paragraph 180 of the  Operational Guidelines ;    \n 5.&nbsp; Reiterates its request  to the State Party to make a clear and unequivocal commitment to eliminate the mining concessions granted within the boundaries of the property (in line with the international policy statement of the International Council of Mining and Metals (ICMM) of not undertaking these activities in World Heritage properties), and those in its periphery, that could seriously and irreversibly affect its Outstanding Universal Value, including the conditions of integrity, if activated; \n 6.&nbsp; Requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1 February 2012 , a report on the inactivity of all mining concessions with a view to their upcoming cancellation, and updating the World Heritage Committee on any other factors significantly affecting the Outstanding Universal Value, including the conditions of integrity, of the property.
