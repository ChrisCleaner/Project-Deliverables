The World Heritage Committee, \n \n  Having examined  Document WHC-15/39.COM/7B, \n  Recalling  Decision  38 COM 7B.79 , adopted at its 38th session (Doha, 2014), \n  Welcomes  the decision by the Supreme Court of Spain to cancel the dredging project to deepen the Guadalquivir River, and  urges  the State Party to make a permanent commitment to cancel the project and not permit any future plans to deepen the Guadalquivir River; \n  Notes with appreciation  the temporary suspension to the issuing of a Unified Environmental Authorization for gas extraction and storage projects in the immediate vicinity of the property, until an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) with a detailed evaluation of the possible cumulative impacts is prepared, and  strongly urges  the State Party to ensure that the potential impacts of those projects to the OUV and integrity of the property are thoroughly assessed, in conformity with IUCN\xe2\x80\x99s World Heritage Advice Note on Environmental Assessment; \n  Notes with utmost concern  that the company M\xc3\xa9xico-Minorbis Group has been proposed by the Technical Committee and the Board of Trade as the international mining company to reopen the site of Aznalc\xc3\xb3llar, and therefore  also urges  the State Party to ensure that a clear risk preparedness plan and associated resources are in place prior to the commencement of mining operations, in order to enable a rapid response in the case of any accidents, and that the potential impact on the OUV of the property is also fully assessed; \n  Also notes  that a Working Group has been established for the elaboration of the Special Management Plan of the Irrigation Zones to the North of the Forest Crown of Do\xc3\xb1ana, and  requests  the State Party to submit the Plan and an EIA to the World Heritage Centre, which fully considers the possible impact on the property\xe2\x80\x99s OUV and ensures the ecological needs for the conservation of Do\xc3\xb1ana National Park are met; \n  Reiterates its request  to the State Party to provide a detailed update on the reported increase in water use for irrigation of rice paddies upstream of the property and reported dam construction on the Guadiamar River and  also requests  the State Party that further research is conducted to confirm the state of the Do\xc3\xb1ana aquifer as noted during the mission, and that regular monitoring of the Do\xc3\xb1ana aquifer be ensured; \n  Further requests  the State Party to implement all the recommendations of the 2015 IUCN Reactive Monitoring mission; \n  Requests furthermore  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1\xc2\xa0December\xc2\xa02016 , an updated report, including a 1-page executive summary, on the state of conservation of the property and the implementation of the above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 41st session in 2017.
