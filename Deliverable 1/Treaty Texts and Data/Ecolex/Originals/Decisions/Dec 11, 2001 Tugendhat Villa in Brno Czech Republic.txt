N&deg;:  1052   \n State Party:  Czech   Republic     \n Criteria:  C (ii) (iv)   \n The Committee inscribed the Tugendhat Villa in Brno on the World Heritage List under criteria (ii) and (iv): \n  Criterion (ii):  The German architect Mies van der Rohe applied the radical new concepts of the Modern Movement triumphantly to the Tugendhat Villa in the design of residential buildings. \n  Criterion (iv):  Architecture was revolutionized by the Modern Movement in the 1920s and the work of Mies van der Rohe, epitomized by the Tugendhat Villa, played a major role in its worldwide diffusion and acceptance. \n The Chairperson stressed the need for an in-depth discussion on the Modern Movement. \n &nbsp;
