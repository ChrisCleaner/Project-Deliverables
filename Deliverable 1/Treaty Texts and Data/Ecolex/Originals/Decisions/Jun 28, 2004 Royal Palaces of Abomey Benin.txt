The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Taking note  of the results of the joint mission undertaken by the World Heritage Centre and ICOMOS, \n 2.  Congratulates  the State Party on its efforts to improve the conservation of the property; \n 3.  Expresses its appreciation  to the governments of Japan, the Netherlands, Sweden, the United States of America and Italy for their generous contributions to the protection of the property; \n 4.  Encourages  the State Party to pursue the implementation of the following measures to enable the Committee to consider removing the property in the List of World Heritage in Danger in 2007: \n a) finalize the national legislative and administrative mechanism for the protection of Cultural heritage in Benin, \n b) to establish new boundarieis of the property and clearly define the buffer zone which aims to protect its integrity, \n c) evaluate and update the conservation and management plan of the property, \n d) pursue restoration and conservation activities to cover at least half of the remaining structural components of the property considered to be in a serious deterioration; \n 5.  Invites  donors who are currently assisting Benin in the protection of Royal Palaces of Abomey to continue, as well as with the support of the rest of the international communities; \n  6.  Decides  to retain the Royal Palaces of Abomey on the List of World Heritage in Danger.
