The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Having examined  Document  WHC-08/32.COM/7A,   \n 2.  Recalling  Decision  31 COM 7A.7,  adopted at its 31st session (Christchurch, 2007), \n 3.  Notes with concern  reports of continued poaching, in particular poaching of elephants, by the army; \n 4.  Expresses its satisfaction  that the State Party has begun the implementation of some of the corrective measures established by the World Heritage Committee at its 31st session (Christchurch, 2007), notably the organization, with support from the World Heritage Fund, of a mixed anti-poaching operation; \n 5.  Requests  the State Party, in cooperation with ICCN, the management body for the protected areas, to urgently pursue the implementation of the corrective measures, and to propose a timetable for their implementation; \n 6.  Reiterates  its request to the State Party to develop, in consultation with the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies, a draft Statement of Outstanding Universal Value, including the conditions of integrity, as well as a proposal for the Desired state of conservation for the removal of the property from the List of World Heritage in Danger, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 33rd session in 2009; \n 7.  Also requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  1 February 2009,  a detailed report on the state of conservation of the property and progress achieved in the implementation of all the corrective measures, with a proposal for a timetable for their implementation, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 33rd session in 2009; \n 8.  Decides  to continue to apply the Reinforced Monitoring mechanism to the property; \n 9.   Also decides  to retain Salonga National Park (Democratic Republic of the   Congo) on the List of World Heritage in Danger.
