The  Seventh Meeting of the Parties  decided in  Dec. VII/24  to request the Technology and Economic Assessment Panel to prepare a report for submission to the Eighth Meeting of the Parties, and present it through the Thirteenth Meeting of the Open-ended Working Group, to enable the Parties to take a decision on the appropriate level of the 1997-1999 replenishment, taking into account amongst other things:     All control measures agreed by the Parties to the Montreal Protocol;   The Report on the Review under Paragraph 8 of Article 5 of the Montreal Protocol;   Historical experience, including limitations and successes, of the phase-out of ozone-depleting substances achieved with resources already allocated, as well as the performance of the Multilateral Fund and its Implementing Agencies;   Special circumstances of low-volume-ODS-consuming countries and small and medium-size enterprises;   Projections included in the 1996 business plan for the Multilateral Fund;   Calculating annual requirements with and without assuming a constant, flat rate of demand (for example, increased demand in some years);   The November 1995 report of the Technology and Economic Assessment Panel on the economic and financial implications of possible methyl bromide and hydrochlorofluorocarbon control scenarios for Parties operating under Article 5;   Relevant decisions of the Seventh Meeting of Parties;   Approved country programmes;     In undertaking this task, the Technology and Economic Assessment Panel should consult with the Executive Committee of the Multilateral Fund and other relevant sources of information.
