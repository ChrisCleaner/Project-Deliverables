The World Heritage Committee, \n \n  Having examined  Document WHC-15/39.COM/7B, \n  Recalling  Decision  37 COM 7B.83,  adopted at its 37th session (Phnom Penh, 2013), \n  Welcomes  the efforts made by the State Party to improve the protection of all components of the property and its buffer zone and  encourages  it to sustain these efforts and to secure the necessary resources and regulatory regimes to ensure no demolition, inappropriate development or deterioration to the heritage buildings which could constitute a threat to the property and its buffer zone; \n  Notes  that the State Party continues to develop regulatory regimes for the property and buffer zone\xe2\x80\x99s protection and  requests  that they are introduced and implemented as soon as possible; \n  Also requests  the State Party to finalize, as soon as possible, the Management Plan for the property, including details of the protective measures provided in its regulatory regimes, and submit it to the World Heritage Centre, for review by the Advisory Bodies; \n  Takes note  of the ICOMOS Advisory mission report and  further requests  the State Party to suspend the possible plans for the reconstruction of the historically lost buildings on the territory of Kremlin within the property, which could represent a threat to its Outstanding Universal Value (OUV), and to:\n \n Submit the approved plan for the demolition of the Building 14, including a technical study on the impact of the demolition on the stability of neighbouring historic buildings to the World Heritage Centre, for review by the Advisory Bodies, \n Review the future plans, in line with the recommendation of the ICOMOS Advisory mission and to submit a project proposal for the vacant area including a Heritage Impact Assessment (HIA), \n Carry out archaeological studies of the area prior to the approval of any future projects and submit the results to the World Heritage Centre, \n Prepare a conservation plan for the property; \n \n \n  Also notes  the number of proposed developments and conservation works, including for the Middle Trading Malls (Red Square) and the Spasskaya and Borovitskaya Towers, and  requests furthermore  to submit to the World Heritage Centre, in conformity with Paragraph 172 of the  Operational Guidelines , technical details, including HIAs, for all proposed projects within the property and its buffer zone that may have a negative impact on the OUV of the property; \n  Finally requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1\xc2\xa0December 2016 , an updated report, including a 1-page executive summary, on the state of conservation of the property and the implementation of the above.
