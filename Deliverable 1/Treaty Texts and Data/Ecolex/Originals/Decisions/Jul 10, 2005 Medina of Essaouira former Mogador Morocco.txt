The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.  Having examined  Document   WHC-05/29.COM/7B.Rev  , \n 2.  Recalling  its Decision  28 COM 15B.45 , adopted at its 28th session (Suzhou, 2004), \n 3.  Regrets  that, in spite of the efforts of the State Party of Morocco, the state of conservation of the Mellah Quarter and the North Wall has worsened, and that new projects having an irreversible impact on the authenticity of the property have been undertaken; \n 4.  Invites  the State Party to inform it, in accordance with paragraph 172 of the  Operational Guidelines , about projects for the current transformation and new construction in the &laquo; protected zone and buffer zone&raquo; so that the World Heritage Committee can recommend appropriate measures to ensure the preservation of the outstanding universal value of this property; \n 5.  Invites  the State Party to define, in collaboration with the World Heritage Centre, an implementation strategy, including the budget for the work, of the project for the conservation and presentation of the Medina of Essaouira presented at its 28th session (Suzhou, 2004); \n 6.  Encourages  the State Party to reinforce the inspection of the Historic Monuments and Sites created at Essaouira and to provide it with adequate human and financial resources to ensure the protection of the property; \n 7.  Invites  a joint mission World Heritage Centre/ICOMOS to assess the state of conservation of the property; \n 8.  Requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1   February 2006 , a report on the implementation of the decisions of the Committee, for its examination at its 30th session (Vilnius, 2006).
