&nbsp;The World Heritage Committee, \n &nbsp;1.  Having examined  Document WHC-10/34.COM/7A.Add, \n &nbsp;2.  Recalling  Decision 33 COM 7A.30 , adopted at its 33rd session (Seville, 2009), \n &nbsp;3.  Regrets  that the State Party did not submit the required state of conservation report for the second consecutive year in spite of the requests made by the World Heritage Committee; \n &nbsp;4.  Takes note  of the letter sent to the World Heritage Centre on 20 July 2010 informing of the decision to set up a new management structure, in full coherence with the National Constitution and the Organic Law of Public Administration in force; \n 5.  Requests  the State Party to inform the World Heritage Committee of the official approval of the new management tool by the relevant authorities, and subsequently submit the new management plan and the related measures to the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies; \n 6.  Also requests  the State Party to invite a joint World Heritage Centre/ICOMOS reactive monitoring mission to the property to assess the progress made in the implementation of the corrective measures aimed at achieving the Desired State of Conservation, and to collaborate with the State Party in finalizing the property\'s Retrospective Statement of Outstanding Universal Value; \n 7.  Further requests  the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by  1 February 2011 , an updated report on the state of conservation of the property for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 35th session in 2011; \n 8.   Decides    to retain Coro and its Port (Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela) on the List of World Heritage in Danger.
