The World Heritage Committee, \n 1.&nbsp;   Having examined   Document WHC-13/37.COM/7B, \n 2.&nbsp;   Recalling   Decision   36 COM 7B.44   adopted at its 36th&nbsp;session (Saint-Petersburg, 2012), \n 3.&nbsp;   Takes note   of the actions implemented by the State Party at the property; \n 4.&nbsp;   Notes with deep concern   the existing conservation conditions, including the recent collapse of historic buildings, and the limited progress that has been made in past years to address them; \n 5.&nbsp;   Urges   the State Party, within the framework of the UNESCO Mali Action Plan adopted on 18&nbsp;February&nbsp;2013, to cooperate with the World Heritage Centre and the Advisory Bodies, as well as any other relevant international bodies, to identify means to implement the existing urban regulations, to update and approve a conservation and management plan for the property and to identify mechanisms to improve synergies among different stakeholders to ensure adequate protection and conservation of the historic fabric and the archaeological sites; \n 6.&nbsp;   Reiterates its request   to the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre a boundary clarification in the framework of the Retrospective Inventory process; \n 7.&nbsp;   Requests   the State Party to invite a joint World Heritage Centre/ICOMOS reactive monitoring mission to assess the state of conservation of the property, in particular the vulnerability of its distinctive architecture, the conditions of the archaeological components of the property and development proposals for different sectors, and to develop an emergency action plan for the implementation of priority conservation and protection measures; \n 8.&nbsp;   Also requests   the State Party to submit to the World Heritage Centre, by   1&nbsp;February&nbsp;2014   , an updated report on the state of conservation of the property and the implementation of the above, for examination by the World Heritage Committee at its 38th&nbsp;session in 2014.&nbsp;
